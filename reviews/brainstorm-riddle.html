Braindom Riddle 2 seems innocent enough. Play through riddles presented on pictures and interact with the picture to show you didn't just guess. After a handful of levels you learn about a different historical figure. You earn points which can be spent on clues or help and there is an overarching story about a man trying to find his mother.

If you were to leave it at that, Braindom Riddle 2 would be a 5/5 in everyone's book but this game is the worst kind of game in mobile gaming today.

Every level requires you to watch 2 ads, one after finishing the level and one after loading a level. You have the option to pay $4.99 not to watch ads, like I did, but then you see how awful this game really is. It isn't a case of solving riddles, it's a case of hoping the riddles will let you interact with them. Strangely enough, the riddles would sometimes not work unless I spent points to get a hint or a clue and then they'd miraculously be working again and there were times where you had to interact with your phone in a unconventional way (shake your screen, turn phone upside down) and you'd need to spend points to learn this info.

In fact, it seems like the game just wants you to spend points and that might possibly be because once you run out, you must pay to get more as each level only gives you a set amount as a reward. That’s right, it’s a cashgrab.

This is the worst kind of game because it exists to only make you spend money, it is what YouTubers call "content dumping" and it is the worst kind of offense to the gaming community.

It's also stupidly easy, if not for the game purposefully making me spend points, I'd get through the entirety of it quickly.

Don’t buy this game. 1/5
