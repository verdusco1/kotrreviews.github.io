For those who don’t know, Dynasty Warriors is a very warn repetitive spin-off of this series, and I must say, the spin-off is better.

The story mode act5s like a long tutorial, where the player receives a watered down version of the story; which is unfortunate as the story could have been this game’s saving grace. There is no “one more turn” feeling because the time structure works like a clock, it’s a very poor choice. Everything that you do takes time, a lot of time. The game has nothing to do for the player while they wait the extremely long time that it takes for time to pass, even when fast forwarding. It’s like a free-to-play game, without anything to speed up the process.

Negotiations and dueling are an intriguing part of the game. They are new and a way of handling speech or single fighting in an RTS, but they get old quickly. Negotiations have two warriors, and there are around 5 options to choose from, but the screen will show one of two events, either the player or enemy thinking or the player screaming something at the enemy. That’s all. Dueling is the same thing only they either just stand there or do one standard attack. The player has 5 choices to see one of two actions play out.

The battles are okay if you can stand the constant waits that are involved to get your city, army, and officers ready for war. This is a waiting game, you watch a circle fill and it takes forever to fill.

Every mission has a time limit and that waiting game takes up a lot of the time involved. Should, because of a lot of luck, the player have enough time to make it to the battle, that timer is the bane of the player. While the player has fun actually maneuvering their army around and creating a good strategy, the time flies straight through, because apparently it takes 3-4 days to walk 1-2 miles. The quick passing of time causes an even faster failure if the player does not rush through each battle.

Don’t get me wrong, the battle system is not new or innovative in any way shape or form, it’s actually pretty lackadaisical. This game is poor at best and complete garbage at worst, but still playable and completable so TeamAggy.com gives War of the Three Kingdoms XIII a 3.4 out of 10.
