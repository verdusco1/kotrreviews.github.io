I do not expect high-quality games on my smartphone, I just expect games that will work on a small screen. This game is different, however, because it was made for Steam and then ported to the phone. That makes me expect a lot more.

The game is incredibly simple to figure out: kill everyone and try not to get caught. There is no learning curve and it does not get more complex than press a kill button or set up a trap that was placed in the level.It adds nothing new or innovative to this kind of genre or gameplay. It's just here and that's about as good as it gets.

The story is badly written; I can see what the dev was trying for but he did not do it well at all. It would be cliche and predictable if it was set up correctly but that's the problem, it's not written like it had been thought out, edited, or outlined. Going through the story, it is easy to see that the ending doesn't make any sense. The reveal is a face palming cliche and has the character coming to a weird justification that was not ever alluded to. The cutscenes are the worst part of the game, with the voice acting being awful and the story being laughable; every cutscene needs to be heavily edited.

The game has many glitches and bugs; I usually lose because a debug took over the screen or I was disoriented by a zoom in/out, and this happens so often. I would gamble that through any playthrough of a level in the game, a debug will have at least happened once. It’s also really hard to lose when that doesn’t happen, you can literally walk through the level killing willy nilly with cops and detectives chasing you the entire time (I tested this and it works).

This game was unsuited for release and does not deserve any praise due to its lack of innovation, the fact that it is not adequate enough to be a cliche, and all around unfinished format. I give this a 1, it’s not worth anyone’s time, it is literally shovelware.
