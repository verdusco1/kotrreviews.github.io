Murdered: Soul Suspect

This game received very poor reviews with little hope; for good reason, it is only just adequate. The game actually tries to bring innovative elements to the investigative-mystery genre.

In this game, the player takes on the role of ? , a felon-turned- Detective trying to catch a serial killer. ? is killed after an altercation with said killer and in order to find peace in the afterlife, he must solve his murder.

Throughout the investigation, there points where the game will give the player options, ex. “What will most likely relate to what you are looking for”, “What will bring you closer to the answer you need”, “What event will help trigger this person’s memory?” In this case it is easy to pick the wrong answer because many of the options would be likely to work. L.A. Noire chose to give multiple ways to get through investigations because of this. Murdered: Soul Suspect should have either followed suit or made choices in investigations more specific.

The game’s sound is fitting, the tones and music and music made the story feel more hopeless yet urgent. Scarier or more thrilling moments were helped by the music , scape scenes felt more flight-or-die.

There are ways to die, as an example, if the main character’s soul gets eaten by a demon, game over. The horror aspect is surprising and unexpected. Often I would slowly creep around the corner during an investigation just to see if a demon was waiting.

Let’s set aside the fact that felons cannot become law enforcement.

The story and minor mysteries (investigations) along the way, while at first are new and intriguing, fall very short. The main storyline becomes bland and cliché while the investigations become chores due to how long the player must patiently wait while they look at every small and insignificant item just to find an obscure reference that is somehow a clue.

After what felt like 10 hours spent only on minor side investigations, I ignore them entirely and focus just on the storyline in order to finish the game.

TeamAggy.com gives Murdered: Soul Suspect a 4.5 out of 10
