I think today, we all going learn the importance of optimization, and how lack thereof can ruin a game.

Now, I was looking forward to this game. It was an interesting premise. It a horror comedy top down and sometimes first person adventure game with puzzles to solve and monsters to avoid. You play a rather interesting (read: absolutely insane) protagonist, A top heavy serial killer in a diaper name Doug. Yes, in a diaper. His only friend is a teddy bear that he talks to. Yep, this game is nuts, as it should be; it takes its cues from HP Lovecraft with its style of horror. This is a weird game with a weird and darkly hilarious sense of humor (just be warned it also VERY offensive at points. Heck the game even starts with warning you about rather racist jokes). Doug finds himself alone and abandoned at the asylum and thus being to check what’s going down at the titular BlackBay Asylum. Well when you not too far from the infamous town of Innsmouth, and also happen to be in asylum in a video game, you pretty much asking for trouble. So now our lovably insane killer must escape some nasty goulies and solve some crazy puzzles to escape the Asylum. However his greatest obstacle in the game is the bane of all video games…. LAG!

What options you get to adjust BlackBay? Why you can change the resolution and whether or not you play in window or fullscreen. That’s it. The beginning intro plays so smoothly too, so why when I start the game proper the game is suddenly as molasses? Guys, this is the kind of thing is what can ruin a game no matter how promising. The game is not exactly a graphics power house but for some reason it gave me terrible lag. Game developers of any skill and experience, please take note. Always, ALWAYS, give the option of adjusting the game’s graphic settings. I was honestly looking forward to Blackbay but it hard to even enjoy anything going at 5 frames per second. I don’t want to be too harsh on Blackbay, it’s an interesting and insane game, but it also insane that I do not have options to adjust the game so it run smoothly even on my low-end laptop. This is a common sense thing to do in games nowadays so not having it is just… crazy, like our diaper wearing inmate. Sorry for the spontaneous rant but I feel it is very important to consider such things when making a game.

UPDATE

So it turns out that the game does have recent issues with Windows 10, giving it poor frame rates. The lack of options can make or break a game.

ART AND SOUND

Oh man, this game is gruesome and gorgeous at the same time. Sure, you can say the graphics look rather old school, but I find it charming. The whole game reeks of atmosphere. The music sends chills up my spine and the sounds paint a haunting landscape. There also some voice acting here, it’s pretty hammy but not exactly the best quality. Eh, at least the dialog is still hilariously absurd.

Setting and Story

For an otherworldly horror story it is very tongue in cheek with its humor. Doug is quite a witty (sometimes) loonie, and ah gosh he’s just so innocent… well, for a serial killer. He does some crazy twisted things in the game, like murder some random crazy chef for his clever... WITH A LAWN MOWER. The times when it not being crazy funny, it’s crazy scary! The monsters in game are quite on the ugly side, and they leave a nasty mess everywhere they go. Doug can also be a nasty mess if he crosses paths with one. Once in a while things…just get weird. Trust me, the first chapter alone will give you a good impression on what madness you will see. It’s not quite for the faint of heart. It manages to both be a scary but hilarious game. Now it’s rare to see humor and horror go so well hand in hand. It makes the already overused setting of a terrible and haunted asylum much more entertaining!

Gameplay

Other than the lag, there is more that bothers me with the game. No option to save. So when you managed to get yourself eaten by a horrible monster or blasted into chunky salsa, you are forced to restart from the last checkpoint. Even worse, you can only save after beating a chapter. That’s not really fair, especially for an adventure game with puzzles of varying difficulty. Imagine playing Dead Space without save stations and the game only saves when you complete a chapter. So when Isaac gets nommed by a Necromorph, smashed by meteors, burned to a crisp or any other hilarious death, you have to start all the back to the beginning of the level, losing whatever progress you have made. You even have to rewatch every cutscene.

As for the actual controls, the game is actually pretty simple. You press space to interact with objects, you got a simple inventory, a journal that gives hints and updates you on your progress (if course all written by Doug’s crazy crazy mind), you walk around with WASD, sprint with shift, yadda yadda. It ain’t fancy but I enjoy the simplicity of it. It makes the game easy to enjoy. Puzzles can pretty hard though but it not exactly hair tearing bad, just needs a good think through.

OVERALL

Despite the lag dealt by Windows 10, I can recognize a good game here. It’s a crazy, nutty, weird, darkly humorous game with a unique charm to it. It’s a perfect game for those who love a good horror adventure to rather your brain with, and maybe wear a diaper for. Okay not really, but it looks on Doug though.

Now that I think about it, the lag didn’t ruin that much for me. Still was bad though, plus there are a few issues, like the lack of a save feature. Oh well.
